CHARTERED TAXATION ACCOUNTANT(Level 2)
 

Eligibility Requirement

 
 

•  Candidate who have successfully complete LEVEL-I of CTA
 

Enclosures with Application

 

 

•  A copy of Mark List of LEVEL-I of CTA
•  A certificate issued by Institute who has Passed Entrance Exams, if applicable.
•  Second Installment of course fees of Rs. 8000/- to be submitted along with application form by    demand draft (or in cash at AKBIFM office only).

 
 

CUT- OFF DATES FOR REGISTRATION

 
 

The registration for entrance exams is open throughout the year. Examinations will be held twice in a year. Hence it is necessary for a student to register at least four months prior to exams. Student will get admission with late fees of Rs.500 up to 15 th FEB/AUG for JUNE/DECEMBER Examinations of current year.

 
 

For June Examination

31st Jan. of Current year.    
For December Examination

31st July of Current year

 

EXAMINATION

   
 

Level-2 examinations will be held in JUNE and DECEMBER every year. A candidate can appear for the examination after satisfactory compliance of requirement mentioned earlier. On-line examinations are conducted through attest centers. Detailed instructions and schedule of examination will be sent to all students in due course of time and also students are required to keep themselves update with the changes and information mentioned on the website regularly.

 
 

RESULTS:

 
 

Results will be declared within 2 months of examinations. Names of the students who successfully complete the respective examination are posted on the website of the Institute. Students are required to keep watch for the results through website.

 
 

CERTIFICATE:

 
 

CTA Certificate will be awarded to those candidate who successfully complete LEVEL-2 together with Industrial Training of Six Months.

 
 

PASSING PERCENTAGE

 
 

•  A candidate will be declared to have passed only if both the stages are cleared.
•  A candidate can either pass both stages at one time and one by one stage.
•  A candidate will have to obtain atleast 40% marks in both the stages.

 
  Restrictions on Number of Attempts  
 

A student will have to Pass LEVEL-II within eight consecutive attempts from the date on which his/her first attempt is due.

 
 

SYLLABUS

 
 

Syllabus of Level – II is designed in such a way to get the detailed knowledge of various field like tax, accounts, Law etc. Level – II also comprise of two stages having four subjects in each stage.

  
  SUBJECTS  
  Stage – III  
 

•  Income Tax
•  Company Law
•  Financial Management
• Banking & Insurance Laws  

 
  Stage – IV  
 

• Special Taxes Incl. FBT, CTT & Wealth Tax
• Corporate Accounting
• Industrial Laws
• International Financing

 
 


ANNEXURE -III

 
 


•  INCOME TAX

 
 
OBJECTIVE 		 
 

To gain knowledge of basic principles of income tax law computation of income under various heads of income.

 
 


DETAILED SYLLABUS

 
 

•  Important definitions of tax act.
•  Basis of charge, residential status and scope of total income.
•  Income which do not form part of total income.
•  Heads of income and various provisions applicable to different heads.
•  Income of other persons to be clubbed with the assessee total income.
•  Set off and carry forward of losses.
•  Deductions from gross total income.
•  Rebate and relief.
   Tax to be deducted at source and collection at source
•  Income tax authority appointment, control, jurisdiction, and powers.
•  Basic previsions concerning procedure for assessment, appears and revision.
•  Assessment of individual, HUF , CO , AOP, firm and cooperative society.

 
  II COMPANY LAW  
 
OBJECTIVE 		 
 

To provide knowledge about the corporate law

 
  DETAILED SYLLABUS  
 

•  Sources of finance (National)- Medium and long term, venture capital, seed capital, equity preference, convertible and cumulative preference shares, Debentures, convertible debentures, hire purchase, leasing, public deposit and institutional finance, life insurance corporation, unit trust of India, industrial credit and investment corporation of India , national industrial development corporation , IDBI , SIDBI, SFC, IRBI. International sources, retained earnings, provisions etc. Issues in raising finance legal form of organization, provisions of the companies Act. Central of capital issue, short term sources, Trade credit, factoring, Bill of exchange, Bank loan, Cash credit, Overdraft, Public deposit SEBI regulations, primary and secondary markets.

 
 

•  Sources of Finance (International) –Raising finals in foreign markets and investments in foreign projects, exchange rate-risk agencies involved & procedures followed in international financial operation-concepts of balance of trade and balance of payment.

 
 

• International finances and risks management- Minimization of political risk, risk of fraud (i.e. travel policy statements), effective recruitment policies and good internal controls especially over procurement and cash; diversifying risk, management of interest rate risk including the use of interest rate swaps, forward rate agreements and interest rate guarantee interpretation of interest option using simple group to show caps, floors and collars. Transaction, translation and economic risk interest rate parity, purchasing power parity and the fisher effects foreign direct investment. Forward contracts and moneyS market hedges currency features and options currency swaps and internal hedging techniques other current international finance issues.

 
 
III FINANCIAL MANAGEMENT 		 
 


OBJECTIVE

 
 

To provide knowledge of various basic concepts of decisions making process in financial management.

 
 

DETAILED SYLLABUS

 
 

•  Meaning, Importance & Features of financial management, Time value of money, Role of     financial Manager.
•  Tools of financial planning & analysis And Ratio analysis
•  Management of working capital, cash & marketable securities management treasury     management, receivables management .
•  Investment decision: Project planning, capital budgeting, Risk analysis decision tree analysis. •      Dividend decision: Dividend policy, its determinations.
•  Financing decisions: Cost of capital, cost of different sources of finances, weighted cost of     capital, marginal cost of capital, concept of types of average.
•  sources of finance ,capital structure and its theories
•  Portfolio management and mergers & acquisitions.
•  Forex management
