CHARTERED TAXATION ACCOUNTANT(Level 1)
 

Eligibility Requirement

 
 

•  Students who have passed entrance exams
•  Graduates who secured 40% or more marks


  Enclosures with Application  

 

•  Attested copy of mark list of Graduation
•  Two copies of recent passport size color photograph
•  A copy of certificate issued by Institute who has passed entrance exams
•  First installment of course of Rs. 8000/- to be submitted along with application form by    demand draft (or in cash at AKBIFM office only).

 
 

CUT- OFF DATES FOR REGISTRATION

 
 

The registration for entrance exams is open throughout the year. Examinations will be held twice in a year. Hence it is necessary for a student to register at least four months prior to exams. Student will get admission with late fees of Rs.500 up to 15 th FEB/AUG for JUNE/DECEMBER Examinations of current year.

 
 

For June Examination

31st Jan. of Current year.    
For December Examination

31st July of Current year

 

EXAMINATION

   
 

Level-1 examinations will be held in JUNE and DECEMBER every year. A candidate can appear for the examination after satisfactory compliance of requirement mentioned earlier. On-line examinations are conducted through attest centers. Detailed instructions and schedule of examination will be sent to all students in due course of time and also students are required to keep themselves update with the changes and information mentioned on the website regularly.

 
 

RESULTS:

 
 

Results will be declared within 2 months of examinations. Names of the students who successfully complete the respective examination are posted on the website of the Institute. Students are required to keep watch for the results through website.

 
 

Restrictions on Number of Attempts

 
 

A student will have to pass Level-1 within eight consecutive attempts given the date on which his/her first attempt is due.

  
 

PASSING PERCENTAGE

 
 

•  A candidate will be declared to have passed only if both the stages are cleared.
•  A candidate can either pass both stages at one time and one by one stage.
•  A candidate will have to obtain atleast 40% marks in both the stages.

 
 

SYLLABUS

 
 

Syllabus of Level – I is designed in such a way to get the detailed knowledge of various field like tax, accounts, Law etc. LEVEL-1 is comprised of two stages having 4 subjects in each stage.

 
 

Stage-1

 
 

•  Practical Accounting
•  Financial Accounting
•  Business Law
•  Central Sales Tax and Value Added Tax

 
 

Stage –2

 
 

•  Auditing
•  Central Excise, Custom and Service tax
•  Cost Accounting and Management
•  Information Technology

VII COST ACCOUNTING AND MANAGEMENT

 
 

OBJECTIVE

 
 

To gain knowledge in respect of cost concepts and detailed procedure of cost ascertainment system and decision-making.

 
 

DETAILED SYLLABUS

 
 

A COST ACCOUNTING (40 MARKS)

 
 

• Basic-Evolution of cost Accounting, Meaning of cost concepts, cost classification, cost     organization and its relationship with other departments, elements of cost and cost     determination, objectives, importance and advantages of cost Accounting, Installation &     Pre- requisite of good accounting system.
•  Cost Ascertainment
•  Material Accounting-
•  Procurement of material stores procedures, preparation of stores ledger & stock utilization.
•  Inventory control and fixation of various levels of stock.
•  Labour Cost-Classification of Labour into direct and indirect depending upon its use in relation     to product, time booking and time keeping in relation to labour. Methods of remuneration,     incentive scheme-monetary and non-monetary labour turnover, treatment of idle time and     measures to control it, treatment of overtime.
•  Direct Expenses-Nature, collection and classification and treatment of direct exp.
•  Overhead-Nature, collection and classification of overheads on different basis, allocation to     cost centres-Primary as well as secondary, Absorption rates of overheads.
•  Methods of costing- Job/Contract costing system, Process and operating costing system.
•  Uniform costing, Inter-firm comparison and reconciliation of cost and financial accounts.
