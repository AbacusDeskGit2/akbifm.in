ENTRANCE EXAM
 

Eligibility Requirement

 
 

•  Candidates who passed 10+2 or equivalent
•  Graduates who secured 40% marks


 

PROVISIONAL REGISTRATION

 

 

               The facility is available to those candidates who have appeared in 10+2 and are awaiting their results. Provisional registration shall be confirmed only if satisfactory proof is furnished with in five months

 
 

Enclosures with Application

 
 

•  Attested copy of mark list of 10+2
•  Two copies of recent passport size color photographs
•  Non- refundable registration fee of RS. 2000/- to be submitted along with application form by    demand draft or on in cash (at AKBIFM office only)

 
 

CUT- OFF DATES FOR REGISTRATION

 
 

The registration for entrance exams is open throughout the year. Examinations will be held twice in a year. Hence it is necessary for a student to register at least four months prior to exams. Student will get admission with late fees of Rs.500 up to 15 th FEB/AUG for JUNE/DECEMBER Examinations of current year.

 
 

For June Examination

31st Jan. of Current year  
For December Examination 31st July of Current year
 

SUBJECTS

 
 

• Fundamentals of Accounts
• Mathematics & Statistics

 
 

The detailed contents of syllabus & recommended books is given in Annexure-I

 
 

PASSING PERCENTAGE

 
 

               A candidate will have to obtain atleast 40% marks in each paper and atleast 40% of the total marks of all the papers

 
 

RESULTS - Results will be declared within one month

 
 
ANNEXURE-I
 
 
I. FUNDAMENTALS OF ACCOUNTING
 
 

OBJECTIVE

 
 

              To develop a conceptual understanding of fundamentals of accounting which includes knowledge of basic terms, bookkeeping & so on

 
  DETAILED SYLLABUS  
 

•  Meaning of basic terms of Accounting, Accounting Concepts and Conventions.
•  Accounting process includes preparation of journal, subsidiary Books, ledger books.

Till the preparation of trial balance and Finalization of Accounts
•  Rectification of errors, Bank Reconciliation Statement and Bills Of Exchange .
•  Depreciation accounting including methods thereof.
•  Accounts of Non Trading Concern- Receipt and Payment Accounts & Income and.

Expenditure Accounts
•  Accounts of Incomplete Records – Single Entry

 
 

II. MATHEMATICS AND STATISTICS

 
 

OBJECTIVE

 
 

To provide basic understanding of important quantitative tools.

 
  SECTION – A MATHEMATICS (50 MARKS)  
 

•  Mathematics of Finance – Simple Interest and Compound Interest.
•  Graph of Linear Inequalities in two variables.
•  Linear, Quadratic, Exponential and Logarithmic Functions..
•  Arithmetic and Geometric Progression Including Series.
•  Simple problems of mensuration

 
 

SECTION – B STATISTICS (50 MARKS)

 
 

•  Classification and tabulation of data.
•  Measures of Central Tendency and Dispersion.
•  Correlation and Regression Analysis.
•  Time series & Forecasting.
•  Index Number
